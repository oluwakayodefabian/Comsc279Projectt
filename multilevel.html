Multilevel Queue Scheduling

Multilevel Queue Scheduling is a cpu scheduling algorithm whereby the process is divided in categories or seperate queues. For example; foreground which is interactive and background which is batch and each queue has its own scheduling algorithm. For foreground, it uses the Round Robin scheduling algorithm while background uses the First-Come First-Served scheduling algorithm. It is efficient for handling different types of processes but it has a rigid structure whereby processes are stuck in their queue

Sample Analogy: Imagine a school cafeteria during lunchtime where the staff serves different groups of people. Teachers have the highest priority and are served first so they can quickly return to their classes. Once all the teachers are served, the staff moves on to the students, ensuring they have enough time to eat during their break. Finally, the administrative staff, with the most flexible schedules, are served last. Each group has its own dedicated line, and people in the same line are served in the order they arrived.
