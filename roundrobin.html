Round Robin

Round Robin is a pre-emptive method of CPU scheduling algorithm where each process is cyclically assigned a fixed time slot. It is simple, easy to use, and starvation-free as all processes get the balanced CPU allocation. A disadvantage about it is that the performance of each process depends on the time quantum size.

Sample Analogy: Imagine a group of children playing a board game. Each child gets a fixed amount of time to make their move, say 2 minutes. Once their time is up, even if they haven't finished their turn, they have to stop and let the next child take their turn. This continues in a cycle until all the children have completed their moves and the game ends.
